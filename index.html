Focused on closing the gap between learning and real-world practice: in curriculum, in tools, and in the systems that connect students to the services they need. Graduate research in connectivism and personal learning environments (2017) is the through-line for everything here. Faculty and students should be able to build and tend their own learning infrastructure, not just consume what institutions provide. Current projects apply that framework to AI-powered tools, course design, and campus-wide AI governance at Glendale Community College. Running alongside all of it is a longer effort to connect student services, career support, and peer networks into the same spaces students already inhabit.

Faculty-Built Tools

Render

A connectivist personal learning environment for students. Seven interconnected modules — profile, goals, job log, resume vault, skills tracker, networking, and interview prep — map to a formal career launch taxonomy. AI-powered via the Claude API; anonymous by design, no PII collected. Built in partnership with GCC Career Services. Pilot planned Fall 2026.

Community & Student Services

Digital Arts and Animation Discord Community

A closed server connecting all Digital Arts and Animation students. Data consistently shows that students who feel connected and supported complete their programs at higher rates. Students can get help in real time, support each other, and share critique in a way that a Canvas discussion board can't replicate — Discord meets students where they already are, on a platform they use every day for gaming and other communities, accessible on phone or tablet anywhere. Currently used for peer critique, announcements, and posting jobs and events. The goal is to grow this into a fuller support network — bringing in career services, advising, library, and disability resources, and inviting industry guests for portfolio reviews and speaker events.

A proposed faculty-led committee at Glendale Community College to build structured AI professional development infrastructure across the institution — connecting faculty, instructional designers, IT, and administrators. Four subcommittees, monthly cadence, co-chair model. Proposal targets the VP of Academic Affairs, deans, and college president.
