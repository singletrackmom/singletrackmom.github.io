Innovation & AI in Higher Ed

Welcome to my sandbox. I'm a Digital Media Arts faculty member with 25+ years in higher ed, exploring what AI and technology-driven innovation can do for student success. These are working prototypes and active experiments, not finished products.

Sometimes the best way to learn something new is to jump in. The tools in creative fields have always been changing, and what survives every shift is the underlying thinking: design principles, visual communication, how people process information. What prepares students for a future that doesn't exist yet is learning to apply those concepts to whatever tools come next, not just the ones we're teaching today.

AI is the latest version of that story and the most significant one yet. Not to replace creative work, but to expand what's possible. If you've ever been told art isn't a practical major, this is the counter-argument. The future belongs to creative thinkers who know how to use new tools. Jump in.

AI Committee Work

Maricopa Community College District
AI Resource Center, Domain 5: Student Support & Success

The Maricopa AI Resource Center organizes district AI work across five domains, each responsible for defining a roadmap and coordinating working groups. Domain 5, Student Support and Success, focuses on how AI can strengthen the systems that help students persist, navigate resources, and complete their programs, from early alert and advising to career readiness and wraparound services. This domain was added in spring 2026; work is just getting started, and I serve as co-chair.

In the Classroom

AVC 183 · Digital Graphic Design 1
Graphic Design History: 1900 to the Present

Each student pair researches one era of graphic design history and builds a tool to teach it to the rest of the class. Everyone becomes an expert on their decade, then learns the other eras from their peers, following a jigsaw learning model. The instructor example covers 1910–1920 and includes a visual slideshow tour and an interactive drag-and-drop sorting game. This is the first time I've invited students to try vibe coding as an extra credit path.

The standard project is a narrated slideshow. The extra credit version is an AI-assisted vibe coding challenge: build an interactive, period-accurate teaching tool from scratch using a prompt-based workflow. No prior coding experience required.

Student projects will be showcased here once submitted.

High School Outreach

DMA Feeder School Newsletter Project

For-profit and private colleges are recruiting in 9th grade classrooms, offering lab access and program perks that require students to commit before they know what the education actually costs. By the time GCC shows up at a college fair, many of those students have already decided. The goal of this project is to get ahead of that by building direct relationships with the people who see students every day: art teachers, yearbook advisors, CTE coordinators, and counselors at our feeder high schools. GCC students win Emmy Awards, place first in district competitions, and transfer directly to ASU. Most students in our own backyard have never heard of us. An AI agent generates periodic newsletters and embeddable graphics for this strategic contact list. Target rollout Fall 2026.

Curriculum Development

Digital Media Arts & Animation · Glendale Community College
Stackable Fast Track Microcredentials

A redesign of the Animation certificate into three stackable Fast Track blocks, each under the 15-credit cap. Students come to GCC from many starting points, some are retooling skills after earning a degree elsewhere, some are exploring college for the first time, and some are still in high school taking a class or two. Stackable credentials give every student a meaningful early win and a clear path forward. Each block counts toward either the Animation & Time-Based Media or Digital Media Arts AAS degree, so nothing is wasted. Curriculum paperwork is underway; implementation expected 2027–2028.
