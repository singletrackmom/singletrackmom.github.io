Session Proposal

From Classroom to Career: Prototyping a Student Dashboard with AI

50-Minute Session · Live Demo · Q&A

Abstract

What if students left your capstone course not just with finished assignments, but with a career launch system they populated themselves — and can keep using after graduation? This session shares the origins, design process, and early testing of Render, an AI-powered career dashboard prototype being developed at Glendale Community College for the Digital Media Arts program.

The need was simple: students in AVC 248 (Design Self Promotion) complete career readiness work across the semester — resumes, job research, networking, interview prep — but when their Canvas access ends at graduation, that work disappears. Render was built to give students a single tool they own, organized around six areas that map directly to course competencies:

  • Job & Client Log — Students paste job descriptions and receive AI-driven skills gap analysis, tailored cover letters, and recommended search strings for LinkedIn and Indeed.
  • Resume Vault — Four-draft revision workflow with AI-assisted editing suggestions tied to specific job requirements.
  • Skills & PD — Software stack mapping, gap analysis drawn from saved job postings, named learning resources, and portfolio project ideas.
  • Networking — Contact log with outreach tracking and AI-drafted LinkedIn messages personalized to each connection.
  • Interview Prep — Role-specific questions generated from saved job descriptions, answer practice with AI coaching feedback, and Big Interview integration.
  • Launch Plan — A personalized 90-day PD plan and weekly schedule generated from everything the student built during the semester, exported as a standalone HTML dashboard they keep forever.

Render is a working prototype — not a finished product. This session walks through why it was built, how it works, and what we're learning from early usability testing with a group of four students and feedback from GCC Career Services. The tool is built in Claude and runs as a single HTML file with no server dependencies.

Attendees will see the prototype in action, hear what's working and what isn't, and leave with ideas for how AI-assisted tools can be designed around student needs rather than handed over as blank prompts. No AI experience required.

~290 words

Learning Outcomes

  1. Describe how a purpose-built AI tool differs from an open-ended prompt — and why that distinction matters for student engagement and equity
  2. Identify at least two ways a structured AI dashboard could support career readiness assignments in their own discipline
  3. Explain the value of iterative prototyping and usability testing when designing AI-assisted student tools
