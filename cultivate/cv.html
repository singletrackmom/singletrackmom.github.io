Research & Professional Interests
Work focused on closing the gap between learning and real-world practice: in curriculum, in tools, and in the systems that support faculty and students. Spans instructional design, UX, graphic design, career readiness, and AI integration. Those threads show up together whether in course design, tool development, or helping faculty think through how technology and AI fit into their teaching. Graduate research in connectivism and personal learning environments, a framework since applied to building AI-powered tools that let faculty and students construct and tend their own learning infrastructure. Particularly interested in how AI changes what is possible in learning design, not just as a content tool but as a thinking partner, and in prompt engineering as a competency that should mirror industry demand. All current courses taught are online, continuously refined over fifteen-plus years, and all tools are built for learners who are navigating education at a distance.

Selected Projects & Curriculum Design

Render, AI Student Career Launch Framework

Glendale Community College · AVC 248 Integration · singletrackmom.github.io/render · 2026–Present

A connectivist personal learning environment for students developed in conjunction with Career Services. Gives final-semester students one place to use compartmentalized AI prompting to create their own projects and learning goals, track job search or freelance work, build their portfolio pipeline, and maintain career infrastructure after graduation. Seven modules map to a formal career launch skills taxonomy. Prototyping in HTML/CSS/Vanilla JS using Claude AI; student pilot planned Fall 2026. Overview →

Cultivate, AI Faculty Professional Development System

Glendale Community College · singletrackmom.github.io · 2025–Present

A connectivist personal learning environment for faculty: one place to manage a professional development plan, sabbatical and conference proposals, career strategy, curated knowledge feeds, and continuing education across a multi-year timeline. Architectured a real-time 62-source RSS news feed with a custom keyword classifier. Includes a full PRD defining user stories, data model, success metrics, and adaptation pathways for team, department, or multi-institution deployment. Overview →

AVC100 Introduction to Digital Media, Course Redesign

Glendale Community College · Launching May 2026 · 2025–2026

Contracted redesign of the department's highest-enrollment course, a 1-credit Digital Media Arts entry point with a documented low student success rate. Redesign grounds every module in retention research; each module directly introduces Student Services including Advising, GCC Cares, Tutoring, and Disability Services, embedding wellness into the learning experience. Built as IMSCC for Canvas import.

Digital Media Arts Discord Community, Student Services Integration

Glendale Community College · In Active Development · 2020–Present

A closed, cross-program community connecting students across all Digital Media Arts courses. Currently used for peer critique, class announcements, job postings, and events, with tutoring access embedded. The longer goal is connectivist: extending the network beyond the classroom into real industry, employers and advisors participating in portfolio reviews, guest speakers from the field, career services and library integrated as active partners, and students building professional relationships as part of their studies rather than after. That outward reach into industry is what makes it a genuine learning network, not just a discussion board. Research and precedent document: Discord Community Building in Higher Ed →
