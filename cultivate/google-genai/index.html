About the Program

Program Overview

The Google Cloud Generative AI Leader certification is designed for people making real decisions about GenAI adoption, not for ML engineers. The training prepares participants for the certification exam through a curriculum built around four connected areas: how generative models actually work under the hood, what Google Cloud's GenAI product stack offers and how those pieces fit together, practical techniques for shaping model outputs through prompt design and grounding, and the strategic questions that surround adoption, from cost to risk to responsible use.

The training is cross-disciplinary by design. Participants come from a wide range of roles and departments, and the conversations are as much about institutional strategy as about the technology itself.

What the curriculum covers

  • Generative AI fundamentals: how models are trained, what they can and cannot do, and where they fit in the broader AI landscape
  • Google Cloud's GenAI product stack, including Vertex AI, Gemini, and the developer tooling around them
  • Techniques for improving model outputs: prompt engineering, grounding, retrieval, and evaluation
  • GenAI business strategy: use case selection, cost structure, risk management, and responsible deployment
  • Certification exam preparation and practice

Why This Training

Why I Signed Up

I am building AI-powered tools for students and faculty, co-chairing the Student Support and Success domain on the Maricopa District AI Resource Center, and teaching students to evaluate AI tools critically. This training rounds out my working knowledge of the major cloud platforms and gets me to a recognized industry credential that speaks beyond higher education.

The Google Cloud perspective is a natural complement to the AI work I am already doing with Claude and other tools. A shared vocabulary across platforms makes it easier to advise students and colleagues who are navigating their own tool choices.
