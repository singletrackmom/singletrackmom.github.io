Months 3–4

PM Core + AI Literacy

PM fundamentals course · Start Inspired · Deep Learning AI
▾
SHAREDComplete the PM fundamentals course. Finish all remaining modules.
SHAREDStart Inspired by Marty Cagan (Kindle ~$20). Read Parts 1 and 2. Map concepts to your GCC experience.
SHAREDWatch Andrej Karpathy's "Intro to Large Language Models" (1 hour). Focus on: context windows, embeddings, tokens, fine-tuning vs. prompting.
SHAREDComplete "AI for Everyone" by Andrew Ng on Coursera (free audit). All weeks.
SHAREDComplete "ChatGPT Prompt Engineering for Developers" on DeepLearning.AI (free).
SHAREDFinish Inspired — Parts 3 and 4.
SHAREDWrite a second PM case study mapping to one of your targets.
SHAREDResearch Gainsight. Watch their free "Customer Success Fundamentals" content on YouTube — useful context for EdTech product teams.
SHAREDEnroll in a free Salesforce or HubSpot Trailhead course. Understand what a CRM record looks like.
SHAREDExplore an open-source API library on GitHub (e.g., the Canvas Python API). Practice reading a README and understanding how an API works.
SHAREDComplete "Building Systems with the ChatGPT API" on DeepLearning.AI (~3 hours). Teaches chained AI calls — directly relevant to how AI features are built in edtech products.
SHAREDFollow one major EdTech conference recap (e.g., OLC, EDUCAUSE). Take notes on what AI features were announced and what problems they solve.

SHAREDWrite a product teardown of an AI edtech tool you use daily. Structure: user problems it solves, likely technical constraints, what you'd prioritize if you were the PM. Portfolio piece.
Months 7–8

Portfolio + Applications

Publish · Practice interviews · Apply
▾
SHAREDPublish something public — a LinkedIn article or detailed post. Frame it as educator → PM perspective.
SHAREDDocument your AI CoP progress so far — a one-page summary of sessions held, faculty reached, and outcomes observed.
SHAREDPropose a PM-framed project at GCC. "I am serving as internal PM for our AI pilot" gives you a current, defensible title to discuss in interviews.
SHAREDPractice PM interview frameworks via Exponent on YouTube (free). Core questions: "Tell me about a product decision you made," "How would you prioritize a roadmap."
SHAREDDo 3 mock interviews on Pramp.com (free).
SHAREDStart applying to roles in EdTech product, instructional design leadership, or AI program management. Use early interviews as research and learning.
SHAREDWrite a 1-page analysis of a learning platform's user experience — what's working, what's missing, what you'd fix first as a PM. Portfolio piece.
SHAREDAttend an EdTech conference (InstructureCon, OLC Accelerate, or EDUCAUSE) if budget allows. PM and product teams are accessible at these events and community visibility matters.
