Module 6 — Networking & LinkedIn

Description

Having a LinkedIn profile is not the same as using LinkedIn to network. This assignment takes you beyond the basics of setup and into active networking — connecting with industry professionals and positioning yourself as a professional rather than a student.

Learning Objectives

By completing this assignment you will be able to:

  • Use LinkedIn's networking tools to connect with industry professionals
  • Engage with content in your field in a professional way
  • Build a presence that supports your job search

Steps

  1. Watch: Learning LinkedIn for Students (free, no login required). Focus especially on the networking sections.
  2. Update your profile to include: a professional headshot, a headline (e.g., '3D Animator | Cinema 4D & Houdini | Seeking VFX Roles'), an About section in first person as a professional, your education, all relevant skills, and links to your Behance, portfolio, and/or Vimeo/YouTube.
  3. Set your job-seeking preferences so recruiters know you are open to opportunities.
  4. Connect with at least 3 classmates, 2 instructors or professors, and join 1 professional group related to your industry.
  5. Take a screenshot of your completed profile and upload here.

Example

Your LinkedIn headline is one of the most important lines on your profile:

&cross; Weak: "Student at Glendale Community College"

&check; Strong: "Motion Graphics Designer | After Effects • Cinema 4D | Seeking Junior Roles in Broadcast & Commercial"
