Learning LinkedIn for Students and Using It to Network

Module 6 — Networking & LinkedIn

Description

Having a LinkedIn profile is not the same as using LinkedIn to network. This assignment takes you beyond profile setup into active networking — connecting with professionals, engaging with content, and positioning yourself as a professional.

Learning Objectives

By completing this assignment you will be able to:

  • Use LinkedIn's networking tools to connect with industry professionals
  • Engage with content in your field in a professional way
  • Build a presence that supports your job search beyond passive browsing

Steps

  1. Watch: Learning LinkedIn for Students (free, no login required). Focus on the networking sections.
  2. Connect with 5 people you do not already know — professionals in your field, employees at studios you are targeting, or speakers from events. Send each a brief, personalized connection note.
  3. Follow 3 companies or studios you want to work for. Turn on notifications for their posts.
  4. Comment meaningfully on at least 3 posts in your field this week — add a specific observation, question, or experience, not just 'Great work!'
  5. Take screenshots showing your new connections, followed companies, and 3 comments.
  6. Upload the screenshots here.

Example

A personalized connection note makes a huge difference:

&cross; Generic: "I'd like to add you to my professional network."

&check; Personalized: "Hi — I saw your talk on rigging for feature animation at SIGGRAPH and have been following your work since. I'm finishing an animation program and would love to connect."
