Leave-Behind Concept

Module 3 — Cover Letter, Elevator Pitch & Midterm Prep

Description

A leave-behind is something you give to a potential employer or client at the end of a meeting so they have something to remember you by. It should be creative, memorable, and consistent with your personal brand identity. This assignment asks you to develop and present a concept before building the final version.

Learning Objectives

By completing this assignment you will be able to:

  • Research leave-behind formats appropriate to your industry
  • Develop multiple concept sketches for a personal leave-behind piece
  • Present your concept on camera and receive peer feedback

Steps

  1. Look online for leave-behind examples in your industry. See the Pinterest link in this assignment for inspiration.
  2. Sketch at least 3 different ideas. Make a paper mock-up to test folding and sizing.
  3. Choose your strongest direction. Your leave-behind must be consistent with your identity brief: same typefaces, colors, and visual language.
  4. Create in InDesign or Illustrator, or use a template if needed.
  5. Post your concept sketches and/or a digital draft to Discord. Give feedback to at least 3 classmates.
  6. Record a short OBS video (1-2 minutes) describing your leave-behind concept — show your sketches, explain your ideas. Upload to YouTube and post the link on Discord and here.

Example

Leave-behind formats that work well for creative students:

  • Mini accordion-fold booklet showing 3-4 portfolio pieces
  • Business card set with a custom holder
  • Postcard with QR code linking to portfolio
  • Custom 3D-printed object with branding
