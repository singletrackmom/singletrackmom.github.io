Cover Letter

Module 3 — Cover Letter, Elevator Pitch & Midterm Prep

Description

A cover letter is your chance to show personality, explain why you want to work for a specific company, and connect your skills to their needs. Many designers skip this step — the ones who do it well stand out.

Learning Objectives

By completing this assignment you will be able to:

  • Write a professional cover letter in standard business format
  • Tailor a letter to a specific job posting and company
  • Connect your skills and experience to an employer's specific needs

Steps

  1. Choose a real job posting — use one from Discussion 2 or 4, a classmate's find, or one you discover. It is okay if you are not fully qualified yet; the practice is what matters.
  2. Review the Cover Letter Sample attached to this module for the correct format: date, company name and address, position name, 3 paragraphs, closing, your name.
  3. Paragraph 1: State the position and why you are interested in this specific company. Show that you researched them.
  4. Paragraph 2: Highlight 2-3 specific skills or experiences from your resume that directly relate to what the job posting asks for.
  5. Paragraph 3: Express enthusiasm, include your portfolio link, and invite them to contact you.
  6. For inspiration, watch this creative cover letter example: Designer's video cover letter for Wieden+Kennedy.
  7. Save as PDF. Post a screenshot of the job posting in the comments of this Canvas assignment.

Example

The Cover Letter Sample is attached to this module. Use it as your formatting template. Paragraph 1: Why this company? Paragraph 2: Why you? Paragraph 3: Portfolio link and next step.

How to Submit
