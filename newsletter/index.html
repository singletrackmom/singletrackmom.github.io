Cover Story · What’s New

The STEAMworks Center is almost open.

Previsualization of the STEAMworks Center XR experience.

A new on-campus makerspace with room for hands-on building and emerging tech alike.

The STEAMworks Center is a new space where students get to make things. Most of it is a makerspace: drill presses, 3D printers, woodworking and fabrication equipment, the kind of tools that let you actually build what you design.

It'll also be home to our work in extended reality (XR, which covers both AR and VR), spatial computing, and AI-assisted workflows. These are areas employers are asking graduates to understand, so we're making sure students have the chance to learn them in context.

Students across every program in our department will have access. The first courses to move into the space include AVC 185 (3D Modeling and Animation I), AVC 200 (Animation and Interactivity), and AVC 240 (Cinematography and Directing for 2D/3D Animation).

Feature · Paid Student Work

Real clients. Real deadlines. Real checks.

Design Studio students working in the media studio.

Students getting paid to make real work for real clients on campus.

The Design Studio grew out of something our students were already doing. They'd pick up design work through a class or a campus internship, building portfolios on projects that needed doing around campus.

Then we wrote a grant. Now it's an actual studio, with paid student positions spanning animation, video, and graphic design, guided by a faculty advisor. They collaborate across disciplines the way real creative teams do, on work that gets seen.

Faculty Spotlight

Twelve years in the making. A Phoenix Film Festival win.

Stephanie Lucas

Film & Media Production Faculty

Film & Media Production Professor Stephanie Lucas and her collaborator Josh Kasselman just won Best Arizona Feature at the Phoenix Film Festival for their first feature-length documentary, Unfinished Work.

The documentary follows her former student Eric Chavez over twelve years, starting with his senior year of high school. Twelve years of footage. One story, told the long way. The kind of project most filmmakers never finish.

Your instructors aren't just instructors. They're the people making the work too.
