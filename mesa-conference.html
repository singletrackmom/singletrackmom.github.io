Session Proposal

From Classroom to Career: A Student Dashboard Built with AI

50-Minute Session · Live Demo · Q&A

Abstract

What if students left your course not just with completed assignments, but with a personalized career launch system they built themselves — and know how to keep using? This session demonstrates a practical, replicable approach to AI-integrated career readiness built around a custom student dashboard developed in Claude.

Drawing from Digital Media Arts courses at Glendale Community College, this presentation walks through three connected assignments centered on a single AI-powered tool: a student career dashboard built using Claude that helps students track and develop their professional readiness across five areas:

  • Resume & Application Tracking — Students use the dashboard to log job postings, track application status, tailor resumes and cover letters, and follow up on contacts — all with AI assistance integrated directly into the workflow.
  • Continued Learning — The dashboard surfaces personalized skill-building resources based on each student's software stack, career goals, and gaps identified through their job search activity.
  • New Projects — Students use the tool to identify portfolio project ideas aligned to real job requirements, then plan and document their work as they build.
  • Networking — The dashboard prompts students through outreach strategies, tracks contacts, and helps them draft personalized messages to industry connections.
  • Interview Preparation — Using Big Interview alongside the dashboard, students generate role-specific interview questions drawn from actual job postings and practice responses tailored to their own background and experience.

Students interact with a tool that was thoughtfully designed for their needs — not a blank AI prompt — which lowers the barrier to entry and shifts the focus to critical engagement with the output rather than figuring out how to use the technology. Assignments are evaluated on the quality of student reflection and evidence of active use, not just submission.

Attendees will see a live demo of the dashboard, receive the assignment framework and rubrics, and leave with a clear understanding of how to adapt this approach for their own discipline. No advanced AI experience required.

~270 words

Learning Outcomes

  1. Describe how a purpose-built AI tool differs from an open-ended prompt — and why that distinction matters for student engagement and equity
  2. Identify at least two ways to integrate a structured AI dashboard into career readiness assignments in their own discipline
  3. Explain how to design AI-assisted assignments so students engage critically with output rather than passively accepting it

Prep Checklist
Before submitting (by March 23): The live demo is your strongest asset — reviewers at practitioner conferences respond to "here's a real tool I built and my students used it." Make sure the dashboard is polished enough to demo live, and note in the proposal that attendees will receive access to the assignment framework and rubrics.

If accepted — what to prepare for May 18:

  • Live demo of the student career dashboard — walk through each of the five sections (resume tracking, continued learning, new projects, networking, interview prep) in real time

  • Shareable link or handout: assignment framework, rubrics, and guidance for adapting the dashboard to other disciplines
  • One or two student examples (with permission) showing how the dashboard shaped their job search or portfolio process
